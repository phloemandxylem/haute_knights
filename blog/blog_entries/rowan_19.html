Dao Studies: Crouching Tiger Hidden Dragon | January 25th, 2019

The tiger is energy and the dragon is spirit. There is a distinction between acquired and primal energy. Immortal Sisters - Thomas Cleary pg. 34

In Daoism, Jing is a term representing primal energy, or the body that we are give at birth. Qi is breath acquired energy. Shen is the human spirit. They are referred to as the 3 jewels within Daosim. Tiger is Mary. It is the feminine yin energy and dragon is the male, YHVH, primal energy and potential for spiritual nature. The bacchic mountain priestesses clothed with tiger skins come to mind.
Tiger is woman, yin, creatrix, movement, life-giver, mercury. The male or dragon, yang, like Shiva, is stable.

Tigers and Ivy symbols of Bacchic mysteries.


These two animals are 2 of the 4 mysterious animals, they participate in a spiritual life-giving process that doesn't depend on gender, but is said to take place within the individual, who has both male and female energy. Genetics is also operating in an X/Y chromosome binary; we have a life-long testament to the male-female communion within our genes.
There is some symbolism between male and female in sexual interaction. There is struggle and compromise between the two generating more mercury - desire, tiger. Desire tiger.
